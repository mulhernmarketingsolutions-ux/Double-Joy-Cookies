Policies & Terms

The Fine Print

The details behind every custom order — payment, timing, delivery area, and a few things required by Oregon's cottage food law. Questions? Reach out anytime.

01Requests & Booking

Custom order requests are currently being accepted for events through December 1, 2026. Submitting the custom order form is a request, not a confirmed booking — I'll follow up personally within 24–48 hours to confirm availability, details, and final pricing.

02Payment

Once your order details are confirmed, an invoice will be sent to you. Your pickup or delivery date isn't reserved on the calendar until that invoice is paid in full — if payment isn't received, the date may be released and offered to someone else. I accept Venmo, Apple Pay, Zelle, and cash — see the Payment page for details, including options for events and commercial bookings.

03Rush Orders

Custom orders typically need at least 2–3 weeks' notice. Orders placed with less than 2 weeks' notice may be accepted at my discretion and will include a $20/dozen rush fee.

04Pickup & Delivery

The custom order form is set up for local pickup around the Portland, OR area only. Local delivery is available on a case-by-case basis for select orders — reach out directly to ask. I'm not able to ship orders at this time.

05Packaging & Gift Add-Ons

All cookies are individually wrapped for freshness as standard. Want yours tied with gift bows? That's available for an additional +$6/dozen — just let me know on the order form.

06Cancellations & Changes

Because every order is made fresh, specifically for you, I ask for as much notice as possible if your plans change. Reach out as soon as you know — cancellations made well ahead of your date are handled far more easily than last-minute ones, especially once ingredients or packaging have already been purchased.

Draft note: exact refund/cancellation terms (e.g. a cutoff window, partial vs. full refund) haven't been finalized yet — this section will be updated once that's decided.

07Allergen Information

Let me know about any allergies or dietary needs when you submit your order, and I'll do my best to accommodate. Please note that cookies are made in a home kitchen that also handles common allergens, including nuts, dairy, egg, gluten, and soy — I can't guarantee any product is completely allergen-free.

08Cottage Food Disclosure

Double Joy Cookies operates as a licensed cottage food business under Oregon state law. As required by Oregon's cottage food exemption:

"This product is homemade, is not prepared in an inspected food establishment and must be stored and displayed separately if merchandised by a retailer." — Required disclosure statement, Oregon Department of Agriculture Food Safety Program
